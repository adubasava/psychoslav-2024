On Perception of Morphological Structure of Belarusian Compound Nouns: 2 Pilot Experiments

Anzhalika Dubasava, Dr., PhD, and Alina Yankavets

moc.liamg@dakilahzna

Introduction

How are compound words processed and represented in the mental lexicon?

Approach 1, full-listing (Butterworth, 1983).
Compound words are stored as separate units. Word recognition does not incorporate the process of morphological decomposition.

support for Modular processing

Approach 2, full-parsing (Taft & Forster, 1975).
Compound words are not stored as separate units. Word recognition incorporates the process of morphological decomposition. The meaning of a compound word is computed every time it is encountered.

support for Interactive processing

Approach 3, combination of approaches above, dual-route models (Caramazza, Laudanna, & Romani, 1988); was not taken into account in this study.

We conducted two pilot experiments to investigate the perception of the morphological structure of Belarusian compound nouns.

The overall design:
  • Step 1 (Experiment 1) Assess whether native Belarusian speakers comprehend the morphological structure of nouns.
  • Step 2 (Experiment 2, Main): Gather and analyze associations with Belarusian noun compounds in the context of the modular versus interactive opposition.

The design of the main experiment was inspired by (Schulte im Walde & Borgwaldt, 2015).
